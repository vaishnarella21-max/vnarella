Context

Tradexa is a B2B technology platform for global trade. As Project Coordinator, I was responsible for ensuring the timely delivery of technical projects and bridging the gap between client requirements and engineering execution.

My Role

Project Coordinator and primary client liaison. I owned stakeholder communication, requirements intake, sprint tracking, and delivery reporting across all assigned accounts.

Tools

Jira Slack Google Workspace Confluence

Goals

  • Ensure 100% on-time delivery for all client-facing technical projects.
  • Reduce ambiguity in requirements before development began , minimizing rework and scope creep.
  • Streamline communication between external stakeholders and internal engineering teams.

Approach

I implemented a structured intake process that required all critical details to be captured and signed off before a ticket moved to development. This alone reduced late-stage clarification requests by eliminating ambiguity at the source.

I established daily stand-ups, weekly progress reports, and a blockers log visible to both the client and engineering lead , creating a shared source of truth that removed guesswork from status updates. By the end of my tenure, the process had become the team's standard operating model.

Outcomes

  • 100% on-time delivery maintained across all assigned projects over 2.5 years.
  • $50K client contract renewed following consistently high delivery standards and relationship management.
  • Reduced rework by ~25% through upfront requirements clarity and structured intake protocols.
  • Improved client satisfaction scores through transparent, proactive communication on every project cycle.
💡
Key Takeaway

Successful project management is 80% communication. Clear expectations and proactive updates prevent most issues before they become critical. A good coordinator doesn't just track tasks , they remove the conditions that create problems in the first place.
